Welcome To Gopalganj::. Gopalganj is a district in the Dhaka division of Bangladesh. The district has about 1,172,415 civilians and its surface area is 1,490 km2. The main city of the district is also called Gopalganj. It stands on the bank of the Madhumati river and located at 23000'47.67" N 89049'2141"E. It is bounded by Faridpur district on the North, Pirojpur and Bagerhat district on the South, Madaripur & Barisal district the East and Narail district on the West. Gopalganj is subdivided into five sub-districts (upazila/thana).

Gopalganj At A Glance:

Today's Gopalganj city has been developed by Madhumati Kol. In the ancient times, this area belonged to the Bengal region. In the Sultani and Mughal era, this region ruled by the Hindu kings. During the permanent settlement (1793), Muksudpur upazila of Gopalganj district was part of Jessore district and the remaining part was in Dhaka-Jalalpur district. In 1807, Muksudpur thana was associated with Jessore district of Faridpur. Jalalpur was the name of a pargana in Faridpur district. Gopalganj Sadar and Kotalipara belonged to Jalalpur Pargana. In 1812 Chandina (Madhumati) river was fixed as a split line of Jessore and Dhaka-Jalalpur districts. Gopalganj-Madaripur area was a huge wetland. Here the prevalence of river-robbery was more. For this reason, the division of Bakerganj was established in Madaripur subdivision in 1854.

A police station named Gopalganj was formed in 1872 in Madaripur subdivision. In 1873 Madaripur subdivision was linked with Faridpur district from Bakerganj district. In 1909, the Madaripur subdivision was formed and the Gopalganj subdivision was formed. Mukadpur thana of Faridpur subdivision with Gopalganj and Kotalipara police station was included in newly formed Gopalganj sub-division.

Gopalganj was the first subdivisional commissioner, Mr. Suresh Chandra Sen. In 1910 the Bench Court of the sub-division was elevated to the Criminal Court. In 1921, the city of Gopalganj was upgraded. According to the census, there were only 3,440 people in Gopalganj city. In 1925, civil court was started in Gopalganj.

In 1936 Muksudpur Thana was formed and Kashiani Thana was formed. In 1974, a police station named Tungipara was formed to break Gopalganj Sadar Police Station. On 1 February 1984, the Gopalganj subdivision was upgraded to the district. Gopalganj district's first Deputy Commissioner was Mr. A F. M. Ehia Chowdhury.
